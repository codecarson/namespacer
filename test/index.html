Coast to Coast Casserole

They’re easy, delicious and presentation-worthy: no wonder casseroles are one of Canadians’ favourite comfort foods. Few dishes are as versatile, since basic recipes can be adjusted to feed more or less and to showcase seasonal ingredients. Another bonus? You can make them ahead and reheat them later. So break out that family recipe and bring it to a potluck! Or try one of these, featuring the best of Canada’s coast-to-coast bounty.

Alex's Atlantic Lobster Mac 'n' Cheese

Alex Weld
Halifax

Alex and her family of five vouch for the decadent richness of this regional treat: “It’s a splurge in terms of expense and calories, but plates are always cleaned, guaranteed. We try to make this casserole when lobster is in season and we can buy it directly from the fishermen at the wharf, but grocery store lobster works, too.”

Alex’s Atlantic Lobster Mac ’n’ Cheese
Makes 8 to 10 servings

Ingredients

  • 4 (1 to 1½ lb/4 to 6 lb total) cooked lobsters
  • 1 cup fresh bread crumbs
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 5 cups whole milk
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2½ cups shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 2½ cups shredded Extra-Old White Cheddar cheese
  • 3 tbsp sherry
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp pepper
  • 1 lb dried cavatappi, gemelli or elbow macaroni pasta

You will need

  • KitchenAid Stock Pot with Glass Lid
  • Farberware Mesh Strainer
  • KitchenAid Lasagna Pan

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Remove meat from lobsters and set aside.

  2. Step 2

    In small bowl, combine bread crumbs and Parmesan cheese. Set aside.

  3. Step 3

    In medium pot, warm milk over low heat (do not bring to a boil). Remove from heat and set aside.

  4. Step 4

    In large heavy-bottom pot, melt butter over medium heat until bubbly; stir in flour. Continue cooking, stirring, until sauce turns light golden brown, about 1 minute.

  5. Step 5

    Gradually whisk in milk; cook, whisking, until sauce begins to thicken, about 3 minutes.

  6. Step 6

    Remove from heat and stir in Gruyère and Cheddar cheeses. Add sherry, cloves, nutmeg, salt and pepper and stir until smooth. Season with more salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Step 7

    Meanwhile, in large pot of boiling salted water, cook pasta one minute less than required for al dente according to package directions. Drain and rinse. Add to sauce and stir to coat. Stir in lobster meat.

  8. Step 8

    Pour into greased 3-quart casserole dish and spread bread crumb mixture over top.

  9. Step 9

    Bake in 375°F oven until sauce is bubbly and topping has browned, about 30 minutes.

Christina's Lasagna in Brodo

Christina Abboud
Beaconsfield, Que.

“Being from a Lebanese and Italian background, I enjoy the cultural diversity of both cuisines. This recipe is one of my favourite Italian ones. My mother learned the recipe from her mother – who learned it from hers – and passed it on to me. It’s an Easter tradition for my family. You know the snow is melting when the smell of Lasagna in Brodo is in the house,” says Christina, now a mom of four herself.

Christina’s Lasagna in Brodo
Makes 12 servings

Ingredients

  • 12 sheets fresh lasagna
  • 1 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Chicken
  • 1 whole chicken
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 stalk celery
  • 1 medium carrot
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 lb lean ground beef
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • ½ cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 Box (900 mL) good-quality chicken broth (optional)

You will need

  • KitchenAid Stock Pot with Glass Lid
  • Farberware Mesh Strainer
  • KitchenAid Lasagna Pan

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Chicken

    1. In large pot, cover chicken, onion, celery and carrot with cold water and bring to a boil. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    2. Reduce heat and simmer until chicken is fully cooked, about 2 hours. Remove from pot and let cool.
    3. Debone and shred chicken. Set aside.
    4. Strain broth and set aside.

  2. Step 2

    Meatballs

    1. Meanwhile, in large bowl, combine beef, egg, Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, and salt and pepper to taste. Shape by ½ tsp into small balls.
    2. In large pot of boiling water or store-bought broth (if using), cook until firm, about 3 minutes. Drain meatballs and discard liquid. Set aside.

  3. Step 3

    Lasagna

    1. In large pot of boiling salted water, cook noodles, a few at a time, until they float to the surface, about 3 minutes. Drain and lay on clean tea towels to cool.
    2. In 9- x 13-inch casserole dish, arrange one-quarter of the noodles in single layer. Cover with one-quarter of the reserved shredded chicken, one-quarter of the meatballs, one-quarter of the Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese and one-quarter of the mozzarella cheese. Pour 1 ladle of reserved homemade broth over top.

    3. Starting with noodles, repeat layers three times, including broth, ending with layer of cheeses. Pour broth over top until even with top layer.
      4. >
    4. Bake in 350°F oven until sauce is bubbly and cheeses have melted, about 40 minutes. Ladle extra broth onto plates when serving, if desired.

Yuki's Campfire Crumble

Yuki Hayashi
Hamilton

“As long as you’ve got delicious fruit and steel-cut oats, there's no such thing as a bad crumble. You can make it any time, anyplace, with fresh local or frozen fruit. I pack the premixed topping for backcountry canoe trips with my family. We buy or pick berries on our way in and cook the crumble on the coals,” says Yuki.

Yuki's Campfire Crumble
Makes 1 to 6 servings

Ingredients

  • 3 cups old-fashioned oats (rolled oats)
  • 1 cup unbleached hard flour
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 pinches nutmeg
  • 1 pinch cinnamon
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, cut in ½ cm cubes
  • ½ tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Approx. 600 g fresh or frozen fruit, including whole strawberries, blueberries, blackberries and pitted cherries, or chopped rhubarb, pears, peaches, apples and pitted cherries (amount varies depending on your casserole dish size)
  • Maple syrup (optional)
  • NOTE: Depending on the size of your baking dish, this recipe may yield more crumble topping than needed. If so, freeze the extra crumble mixture to use as a shortcut another day.

You will need

  • KitchenAid Stock Pot with Glass Lid
  • Farberware Mesh Strainer
  • KitchenAid Lasagna Pan

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    In large bowl, combine oats, flour, sugar, nutmeg and cinnamon.

  2. Step 2

    Carefully drop in butter. Using your hands, gently stir until thoroughly combined. Do not mush the butter: keep it cubed.

  3. Step 3

    Drizzle vanilla over top.

  4. Step 4

    Spread fruit into 2-inch layer in bottom of casserole dish, group of small ramekin-style mini casserole dishes (or, if cooking over a campfire, into a camp frying pan with lid, to keep ashes out). If fruit isn’t sweet, drizzle maple syrup over top (if using). (Rhubarb will require more aggressive sweetening.)

  5. Step 5

    Spread 1- to 1½-inch layer of oat mixture over top, evenly distributing butter.

  6. Step 6

    Bake in 375°F oven (or on bed of glowing coals, if cooking over a campfire) until fruit juice is bubbly and topping has browned. Baking time varies depending on casserole dish size, but about 30 minutes is average for a 2 litre rectangular glass casserole dish.

Elizabeth's Spring Vegetable Casserole

Elizabeth Spring
North Saanich, B.C.

Elizabeth blogs about healthy, athlete-inspired recipes at GuiltyKitchen.com. The mom of two likens this gluten-, soy-, refined sugar- and legume-free dish to mashed potatoes, “only better.” She uses local, seasonal squash, celery root and cauliflower, plus hazelnuts. “Hazelnuts grow right here on Vancouver Island and are amazing roasted with a little butter and salt. Yum!” says Elizabeth.

Elizabeth’s Spring Vegetable Casserole
Makes 4 to 6 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 medium delicata squash or acorn squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 small celery root, peeled and cubed
  • Half small cauliflower, cut into small florets
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter) or unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • ¼ tsp ground thyme
  • ¼ to ½ tsp sea salt
  • Pepper to taste
  • 1 large egg or ¼ cup egg whites
  • ½ to 1 tsp truffle oil
  • Hazelnut topping
  • 1 cup crushed hazelnuts
  • 2 tbsp melted ghee (clarified butter) or unsalted butter
  • ¼ to ½ tsp sea salt

You will need

  • KitchenAid Stock Pot with Glass Lid
  • Farberware Mesh Strainer
  • KitchenAid Lasagna Pan

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Hazelnut topping

    Stir together hazelnuts, melted ghee and salt. Set aside.

  2. Step 2

    Casserole

    1. In steamer, cook squash, celery root and cauliflower until soft enough to poke through with fork, about 15 minutes.
    2. In large bowl, using hand blender, purée vegetables with ghee, mustard, thyme, salt, and pepper to taste; let cool slightly, about 3 minutes. (Alternatively, use a large-capacity countertop blender.)
    3. Add egg and purée until evenly combined. Pour into greased 1.5 litre casserole dish and spread hazelnut topping over top.
    4. Bake in 400°F oven until golden, about 25 minutes. Before serving, drizzle truffle oil over top.
